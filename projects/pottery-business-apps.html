Mud Goods

I have been working behind the scenes of my wife's new pottery business. First, there was a work tracking utility. It helps her keep records of all of her pots that are in progress, move them through different stages and attach photos. I recently transitioned it from using IndexedDB to an Azure Cosmos database so she can work both between her phone and laptop. I've also made huge strides in performance upgrades so everything runs quickly and smoothly. My live site is password protected but the code is on GitHub.

Next was a quick and clean payments app for customers at art markets. They scan a QR code and have three options to click through and pay.

Coming soon, is her store front. In my local version, the tracking app is built out to allow her to publish listings directly and the storefront can only read listings from the Azure Cosmos DB, not write or access anything else. Security practices are top of mind for me in this suite of apps.
